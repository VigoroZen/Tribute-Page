Issac Newton

The man who discovered gravtiy and a father of physics.

Isaac Newton, the brilliant physicist and mathematician, revolutionized our understanding of the universe with his laws of motion and gravity and invented calculus.

Here's 10 Time Stamps of Newton's life:

  • 1643 - Issac Newton was born on January 4th in Woolsthorpe, Lincolnshire, England
  • 1661 - He was admitted to Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1665 - Issac discovers the Method of Fluents.
  • 1665 - Newton begins to create what we know as calculus
  • 1665/6 - Newton discovers gravity and the laws of universal gravitation.
  • 1666 - Issac experiments with light and color during the plague.
  • 1668 - Newton invents the reflecting telescope.
  • 1687 - Newton publishes the "Mathematical Principles of Natural Philosophy" in which he reveals his 3 laws of motion, along with many other theories and formulae that impacted Science and Mathematics greatly.
  • 1701 - Issac publishes his law of cooling.
  • 1727 - Newton dies on the 31st of March in Kesington, London aged 84.
